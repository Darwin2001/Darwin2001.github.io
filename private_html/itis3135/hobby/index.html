What is competitive Pokemon battling?

Competitve Pokemon battling stems from the popular kids game Pokemon. Competitve battling is simply taking the battling aspect of the game and turning it into a competition, with people all over the world training to become better and taking the game to it's limit.


Pokemon battling can be simply broken down into two phases. The trainer and the pokemon. Pokemon being a predictive game just as much as a reactionary sometimes it isn't the pokemon that beat you but the trainer themselves like in chess. It isn't the pawn or rook that beat you but the player instead. This website will cover the pieces themselves, what makes certain pokemon better and what gives them an edge over others.

Stats, IVs, and EVs

When it comes to Pokemon there are various things that set certain pokemon apart from each other. While they look different and are named different, the stat totals are what we look at when we decifer what pokemon to use.


Stats

A pokemon has 6 stat areas where stat points can be allocated: HP, Attack, Defense, Sp.Attack, Sp.Defense, and Speed. HP is health points which determine how many hits your pokemon can take. Attack and Sp.Attack are attacking stats that help determine what how much damage a pokemon takes. Defense and Sp.Defense determine how much damage your pokemon is going to take from attacks. Finally, speed determines which pokemon moves first.

A base stat total is the number of points a pokemon has towards all stats which can differ, generally pokemon with higher base stat totals are better than ones who don't have one


IVs

Pokemon have IVs also know as Individual values. These points can be spent on the same 6 stat totals and are assigned automatically to a pokemon. These values are initially hidden to the player and need a special in game item to be shown. These hidden values explain why a pokemon of the same species and level might have faster speed than the other.


EVs

IVs are similar to EVs in that they both add to a pokemon stats and can provide an advantadge over other pokemon of the same speices. known as Effort Values, EVs can be earned depending on the type of pokemon your pokemon defeates in training as well as the type of held item they use.

Abilities and Movepools

The next things to cover are going to be a Pokemon's held item and movepool. These two things go hand in hand to help execute what the pokemon wants to do as well determine how viable a pokemon is.


Abilities

Pokemon abilities, introduced in generation 3 of the video games are an passive move the pokemon can execute which is most of the times advantadgeous but can sometimes be detrimental. Abilities like Intimidate and Sunny Day are hailed across the community as they lower attack and set up a sun weather effect respectively, two things that used to require moves to do but are now automatically done when the pokemon switches in.


Movepools

Movepools determine the moves pokemon can learn. Movepools can make or break the the pokemon's competitve viability as a good pokemon that only learns bad moves is no more viable than a bad pokemon that learns good moves. Pokemon's with a more diverse movepool are typically more viable then a pokemon with a strict movepool, although both have their uses.

Held items

The final item to be broached is held items. Pokemon use held items in order to give them an outside advantadge that doesn't require the use of a move. Items like leftovers recover the users health at the end of a turn, while some items like escape tag gurantee a pokemons ability to run away after a battle. This is important as it can allow a pokemon another advantadge over a pokemon it would typically not have an advantadge again.
